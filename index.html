The Big Idea

Every date from 1900 to 2099 lands on a fixed day of the week, and you can work it out with simple addition — no calendar needed. You'll do it in three short steps:

Part 1 uses the day and month, Part 2 uses the year. In each part you add two numbers, then find the remainder — how much is left over after taking away the largest multiple of 7 that fits (0, 7, 14, 21…). That leftover is always a number from 0 to 6. Keep the two leftovers in mind, and in Part 3 you add them and take the remainder one last time. The final number (0–6) tells you the day — see the key below.

🗓 Century rule: 1900–1999 → as-is. For 2000–2099, subtract 1 inside Part 2 before finding the remainder. E.g. 2024 → 24 + 6 − 1 = 29.
🔄 Leap year: in a leap year, January → 0 (not 1) and February → 3 (not 4). E.g. Feb 2020 uses 3, not 4.
